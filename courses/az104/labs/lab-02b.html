Lab 02b 🟡 Intermediate ⏱ 30 min

Governance via Azure Policy

Create and assign policies, manage compliance, and configure resource locks

Module 2 — Manage Identities and Governance

Lab Objectives

  • ✓Create and assign a built-in Azure Policy definition
  • ✓Create a policy initiative (set) from multiple definitions
  • ✓Evaluate compliance state and trigger an on-demand scan
  • ✓Apply resource locks to prevent accidental deletion

Prerequisites

  • •Azure subscription with Owner or Policy Contributor role
  • •Resource group az104-rg1 in East US

Lab Steps

Architecture Diagram
1

Explore built-in policy definitions

Portal

Search for Policy in the portal. Go to Definitions and filter by category Compute. Review Allowed virtual machine size SKUs.

2

Assign the allowed SKUs policy

Portal

In Policy → Assignments → + Assign policy, click the scope selector and choose az104-rg1. Search for Allowed virtual machine size SKUs and select it. Click Next → Parameters. Enter Standard_B1s, Standard_B2s, Standard_B4ms as allowed SKUs. Click Review + create → Create.

3

Create a policy initiative

Portal

In Policy → Definitions → + Initiative definition, create an initiative named AZ104-Governance and add the Allowed locations and Require a tag on resources definitions.

4

Trigger a compliance scan

Portal

In Policy → Compliance, click the Re-evaluate Compliance button in the toolbar. Wait 2–3 minutes and refresh. The dashboard updates to show the current compliance state of az104-rg1.

5

Apply a resource lock

Portal

Open az104-rg1 → Settings → Locks → + Add. Set Name to DoNotDelete, Lock type to Delete. Click OK. The lock now prevents deletion until it is removed.

6

Verify policy enforcement

Portal

Try to create a VM with size Standard_D4s_v3 in az104-rg1. The deployment should fail with a policy violation error.

Validation Checklist

  • ✓AllowedVMSKUs assignment appears on the resource group IAM/Policy blade.
  • ✓Compliance scan shows the policy assignment in Compliant or Non-compliant state.
  • ✓Resource lock DoNotDelete appears under Settings → Locks on the resource group.
  • ✓Deploying a disallowed VM SKU returns RequestDisallowedByPolicy error.

Cleanup Resources

Run after the lab to avoid ongoing charges.

# Remove the lock first (required before deleting resources)
az lock delete --name "DoNotDelete" --resource-group az104-rg1
# Remove the policy assignment
az policy assignment delete --name "AllowedVMSKUs" --resource-group az104-rg1
